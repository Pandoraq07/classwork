ABOUT THE SDA CHURCH

The SDA Church, or Seventh-day Adventist Church, is a Protestant Christian denomination known for its observance of Saturday as the Sabbath, and for its emphasis on the Second Coming of Jesus Christ.

ORIGIN

It was founded in 1863 in the United States. It grew out of the Millerite movement of the 1840s, which predicted the Second Coming of Christ. Key figures include:

  • WILLIAM MILLER (PREDECESSOR)
  • ELLEN G. WHITE (PROPHETESS, CO-FOUNDER)

CORE BELIEFS

The church believes in the Bible and is therefore a Bible-based church. It also believes in health laws and healthy living principles, the state of the dead, the sanctuary doctrine, and most importantly, the second coming of Jesus Christ.

GLOBAL PRESENCE
  • Membership: Over 22 million members worldwide.
  • Institutions: Thousands of schools, hospitals, and publishing houses.
  • Missions: Present in over 200 countries.
